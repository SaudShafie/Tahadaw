Team Tahadaw
Welcome — this is Tahadaw, which in Arabic means “exchange gifts.” The name comes directly from the prophetic hadith “Tahadaw tahabbu” — give gifts and you will love one another. That idea drives the whole product. Tahadaw is not an online store; it is an AI-assisted gift-planning platform that helps someone go from “I don’t know what to give” to a thoughtful, real, chosen gift. The Problem

Giving a good gift is harder than it should be.

  • Stressful & slow — choosing the right gift eats time and mental energy.
  • We forget & repeat — no memory of what we already gave each person.
  • Generic sites push products, not thoughtful matches to the person.
  • Occasions slip by, and messages or presentation feel impersonal.
Everyone here has felt this. Gift-giving is emotionally loaded but the tools are bad — search engines and shops optimize for selling products, not for the relationship. People forget what they gave last year and repeat gifts, they miss occasions, and the final delivery feels generic. Tahadaw attacks all four of these problems.

What Makes It Different

Ideas first. Real products second.

  • Not a store — Tahadaw never pushes a catalog at you.
  • AI proposes gift ideas tailored to the person and occasion.
  • Only after you pick an idea do we search for a real product.
  • A reusable “memory of people” makes every future suggestion better.

Idea engine

Thoughtful, explainable ideas — with emotional & practical fit — before any shopping.

Then the product

The chosen idea drives a focused product search — and only that pick is saved.

This is the heart of why Tahadaw is different. Most “gift finders” are just product search. We separate the idea from the product: the AI reasons about what would be a meaningful gift first, and we only go shopping once the user commits to an idea. This keeps suggestions personal instead of commercial, and it’s why the recipient profile — the memory of people — is so central.

Status-machine enforcement: the backend validates the plan’s status at every step, so a user can’t jump from “created” straight to “product” — each stage gates the next.

This is the spine of the application: a GiftPlan moves through eight states, and the backend enforces the order with a status machine. You answer the admin’s required questions, the AI generates and you answer follow-up questions, the AI produces ideas, you select one, we find a real product, and the plan completes into gift history. The key engineering point is that steps cannot be skipped — the status gate guarantees a clean, predictable flow.
AI in Tahadaw · one engine, many features

A single, central AiService.

  • Every AI feature calls one AiService.ask(prompt) (OpenAI gpt-4o-mini).
  • Admin required questions + AI-generated follow-ups tailor each plan.
  • JSON responses parsed into typed DTOs via AiJsonParser.
  • Standalone Gift Quality Check scores any idea’s suitability.

Each AI gift idea returns

The searchKeyword is what drives the real product search on the next step.

A design decision we’re proud of: rather than scattering OpenAI calls everywhere, all AI goes through one central AiService.ask(prompt) using gpt-4o-mini. Each domain service builds a prompt, asks once, and AiJsonParser turns the JSON into typed DTOs. Every gift idea comes back as structured fields — including a searchKeyword that feeds the product search — and there’s a standalone quality check that can score any idea against a recipient.

Once an idea is selected, we use its searchKeyword to query SearchAPI.io (Google Shopping) for real, purchasable products. An important data decision: we never store the whole result set — only the single SelectedProduct the user actually chooses is saved. That keeps the database clean and the gift plan focused on a real, concrete gift.

Gift Card
The gift card is fully generated on the server. We build a QR code with ZXing, render it into a card image, and — importantly for deployment — we store both the QR and the card image as LONGBLOB bytes inside MySQL rather than on the local disk. That makes the app stateless, which matters when we scale on AWS. The card can be viewed as a PNG and is emailed to the recipient through Spring Mail.

Group gifts let several people choose together. An organizer creates options — or has the AI generate them — then sends secure, token-based invites. The backend is a clean layered architecture: controllers stay thin, services hold the business rules and the status machine, repositories are Spring Data JPA, and everything maps to 22 MySQL entities. We separate DTO IN (validated input) from DTO OUT, and a centralized ApiException handler standardizes errors. Outbound calls to OpenAI, SearchAPI, Moyasar, Twilio, and mail go through WebFlux WebClient. The stack is Java 17 and Spring Boot 4.1, with ModelMapper, Lombok, ZXing, and OpenPDF.

Deployment is designed around AWS. The app is already containerized with a multi-stage Dockerfile, and crucially it’s stateless — because images live in MySQL rather than on disk, we can run multiple containers behind a load balancer. The path is: build the image, push to ECR, run on ECS/Fargate (or Elastic Beanstalk), with Amazon RDS for MySQL. Secrets are environment variables, and we bundled the Amiri fonts plus headless AWT so Arabic gift cards and PDFs render correctly on a slim Linux host. The container is done; the AWS rollout is the next step.
